# Learning (learn.html)

*Structure: Ponder / Practice / Encourage — a three-step enculturation journey.*

---

## Step 1: Ponder

### How much of your life is already a commons?

The air you breathe. The language you speak. The knowledge passed down by people you'll never meet. Commoning isn't something new to learn — it's something to remember.

And yet so much of what we share — governance frameworks, coordination patterns, field guides, research — stays buried inside organizational silos. Rebuilt, duplicated, lost. What would change if it were gathered in one place?

Not owned. Not extracted. Tended — like a shared field.

---

## Step 2: Practice

### Collective tending is a muscle.

Commoning is a practice — a way of being, not just a structure. It asks us to change our priorities: to share before we're asked, to contribute back what we've learned, to tend what we didn't plant.

This isn't a platform to consume. It's a living collection — knowledge, governance tools, coordination patterns, brand resources — that grows as more people share what they've built and draw on what others have offered.

The choice to tend is intrinsic. No one mandates it. But the practice changes everything.

---

## Step 3: Encourage

### We each have a role to play.

There are ripples we'll never get to see. Seeds we plant for future generations. Work that outlasts the organizations that made it.

The regenerative movement doesn't need another platform. It needs shared ground — where your knowledge stands beside others', where experiments can fail safely and be learned from, where the invisible labor of coordination is finally made visible.

You don't have to carry everything alone. Let's tend to this together.

# Join the Commons (join.html)

*Structure: 4-step form — Who you are / What you tend / What you bring / Your commitment. The form is the threshold crossing.*

---

## Step 1: Who are you?

The commons is held by people and organizations. Tell us who's arriving.

- **Joining as:** Individual / Organization (toggle)
- **Your name** (required)
- **Organization name** (if org)
- **Your role** (if org)
- **Email** (required)
- **Where are you based?**

---

## Step 2: What do you tend?

We don't need a CV. We want to understand the ground you're already working.

- **Describe your regenerative work** — What are you building, growing, researching, or repairing?
- **What areas does your work touch?** — Ecological restoration, Regenerative agriculture, Governance & coordination, Regenerative finance, Community building, Education & knowledge, Technology & infrastructure, Indigenous & traditional knowledge, Health & wellbeing, Arts & culture, Policy & advocacy, Other
- **How did you find the commons?**

---

## Step 3: What do you bring?

The commons grows through what members offer. Not everything at once — just what feels right to begin with. Contribution before extraction is how this works.

- **What could you offer to the commons?** — Knowledge, frameworks, datasets, governance templates, coordination patterns, research, practice — anything that others in the movement could build on.
- **What commons categories would you contribute to?** — Knowledge, Infrastructure, Governance & Legal, Coordination, Brand & IP
- **What would you tend here?** *(threshold offering)* — What brought you to this ground? What do you hope becomes possible when people share what they know?

---

## Step 4: Your commitment

Membership is a shared commitment. These are the principles that hold the commons together. Read them. If they ring true, this is your place.

**Seven Values:**
- **Care** — We tend to the commons and to each other.
- **Stewardship** — We hold the commons in trust for those who come after us.
- **Transparency** — We iterate in public. We name our developmental stage honestly.
- **Reciprocity** — Benefits flow back to those who nurture the commons.
- **Plurality** — We honour many expressions of regenerative practice.
- **Collaboration** — We build with, not for.
- **Integrity** — We are what we say we are.

**Agreements:**
- [ ] I align with these values and commit to upholding them as a member of Regen Commons.
- [ ] I commit to reciprocal contribution — sharing back knowledge, practice, or resources.
- [ ] I understand that commons assets are shared under a CopyFair license — free use within the commons, with derivative works contributed back.
